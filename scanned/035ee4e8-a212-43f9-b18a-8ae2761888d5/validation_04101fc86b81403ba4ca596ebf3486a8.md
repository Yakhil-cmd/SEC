### Title
SNS Governance `split_neuron` Inherits Age Bonus, Enabling Secondary Market for Aged Neuron Positions - (File: `rs/sns/governance/src/governance.rs`)

### Summary
In SNS governance, `split_neuron` creates a child neuron that unconditionally inherits the parent's `aging_since_timestamp_seconds`. Combined with the `AddNeuronPermissions` / `RemoveNeuronPermissions` mechanism (which allows granting `Split` and `ManagePrincipals` to arbitrary principals), any SNS neuron holder can effectively sell their accumulated age bonus to a buyer without the buyer having to wait the required time period. This directly mirrors the xINTX secondary market bypass: the `split()` analog creates a specific-quantity child position, and the permission-transfer analog replaces the ERC-20 `transfer()`.

### Finding Description
`split_neuron` in `rs/sns/governance/src/governance.rs` constructs the child neuron struct at lines 1364–1381 with `aging_since_timestamp_seconds: parent_neuron.aging_since_timestamp_seconds`. The child neuron therefore carries the parent's full accumulated age, which directly feeds into the age bonus multiplier in `voting_power()` (`rs/sns/governance/src/neuron.rs`, lines 224–231). The age bonus can be up to `max_age_bonus_percentage` (typically 25%) of voting power, accruing over `max_neuron_age_for_age_bonus` (typically 4 years).

The SNS permission system (`AddNeuronPermissions`, `RemoveNeuronPermissions`) allows the original neuron owner to grant `Split` and `ManagePrincipals` to any third-party principal. When an SNS is initialized via `SnsInitPayload::get_nervous_system_parameters()`, `neuron_grantable_permissions` is set to `all_permissions` by default (`rs/sns/init/src/lib.rs`, line 814), making all permissions grantable in practice.

The child neuron also inherits `dissolve_state` and `voting_power_percentage_multiplier` from the parent, and starts with zero `neuron_fees_e8s` (fees are not inherited), giving the buyer a clean slate with the parent's full time-based bonuses.

### Impact Explanation
A buyer can acquire SNS voting power with a full 4-year age bonus (up to 25% extra voting power) without waiting 4 years. This:
- Undermines the age bonus incentive, which is designed to reward long-term commitment to the SNS
- Allows disproportionate voting power and voting rewards to be purchased on a secondary market
- Distorts SNS governance outcomes, since voting power is supposed to reflect genuine long-term stake

The NNS `split_neuron` has the same age-inheritance behavior (`rs/nns/governance/src/governance.rs`, line 2245: `parent_neuron.dissolve_state_and_age()`), but NNS neurons are harder to "transfer" because the controller is fixed to the caller and hot keys have limited permissions. The SNS permission model is the necessary vulnerable step.

### Likelihood Explanation
Medium. The attack requires deliberate coordination between a seller and buyer, but:
- The `manage_neuron` endpoint is publicly reachable by any ingress sender
- No protocol-level mechanism prevents granting `Split` + `ManagePrincipals` to a third party
- SNS deployments initialized via `SnsInitPayload` have all permissions grantable by default
- The sequence (grant permissions → split → remove seller permissions) is entirely on-chain and requires no privileged access

### Recommendation
When `split_neuron` creates a child neuron, reset `aging_since_timestamp_seconds` to the current timestamp (i.e., treat the child as a newly staked neuron with zero age). This is consistent with how `disburse_to_neuron` in NNS governance creates a child with a fresh age (`aging_since_timestamp_seconds: created_timestamp_seconds`). Alternatively, if age inheritance is intentional for the same controller, enforce that the child neuron's permissions are restricted to the same principal set as the parent at split time, preventing permission-based ownership transfer of the child.

### Proof of Concept

**Entry path:** Unprivileged ingress call to SNS governance canister `manage_neuron` endpoint.

**Step-by-step:**

1. **User A** has an SNS neuron staked for 4 years (`aging_since_timestamp_seconds = now - 4_years`), giving maximum age bonus (25% extra voting power). User A wants to liquidate this position.

2. **User A** calls `manage_neuron` → `AddNeuronPermissions` to grant User B's principal `Split` and `ManagePrincipals` permissions on the neuron. This succeeds because `neuron_grantable_permissions = all` in standard SNS deployments. [1](#0-0) [2](#0-1) 

3. **User B** calls `manage_neuron` → `Split` with `amount_e8s = desired_amount`. `split_neuron` creates a child neuron: [3](#0-2) 

   The child neuron receives `aging_since_timestamp_seconds: parent_neuron.aging_since_timestamp_seconds` — the full 4-year age — at line 1370.

4. **User B** calls `manage_neuron` → `AddNeuronPermissions` on the child neuron to add themselves as sole `ManagePrincipals` holder, then calls `RemoveNeuronPermissions` to remove User A.

5. **Result:** User B now controls a child neuron with 4 years of age bonus. The age bonus feeds directly into voting power: [4](#0-3) 

   User B receives up to 25% more voting power and proportionally more voting rewards than a freshly staked neuron of the same size, without having waited 4 years. User A received payment off-chain and avoided the need to dissolve (no dissolve delay penalty). Neither party paid any protocol-level fee for this transfer.
