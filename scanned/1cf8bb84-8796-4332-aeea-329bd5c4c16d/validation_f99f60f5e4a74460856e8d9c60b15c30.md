### Title
Partial ICP Deposit Not Automatically Refunded in SNS Swap - (`rs/sns/swap/src/swap.rs`)

### Summary
The SNS Swap canister's `refresh_buyer_token_e8s` function accepts only a partial amount of the ICP a buyer has deposited (capped by the swap's `max_direct_participation_icp_e8s` or `max_participant_icp_e8s` limits), but does not automatically return the excess ICP to the buyer. The excess ICP is locked in the buyer's subaccount on the swap canister for the entire duration of the OPEN phase, and is only recoverable after the swap closes (COMMITTED or ABORTED) via a separate, non-automatic `error_refund_icp` call. The codebase itself acknowledges this gap with a TODO comment.

### Finding Description

In `rs/sns/swap/src/swap.rs`, the `refresh_buyer_token_e8s` function reads the buyer's ICP balance from the ledger and caps the accepted participation at two limits:

1. The global remaining capacity (`max_increment_e8s = self.available_direct_participation_e8s()`)
2. The per-participant cap (`max_participant_icp_e8s`) [1](#0-0) 

The function returns a `RefreshBuyerTokensResponse` where `icp_accepted_participation_e8s` may be less than `icp_ledger_account_balance_e8s`, but the excess ICP sitting in the buyer's subaccount is **not returned**: [2](#0-1) 

The codebase explicitly acknowledges this as an unresolved issue: [3](#0-2) 

The only recovery mechanism is `error_refund_icp`, which:
- Is only callable after the swap reaches ABORTED or COMMITTED state (not during OPEN)
- Must be called proactively by the buyer (or anyone on their behalf)
- Is not triggered automatically [4](#0-3) 

The `error_refund_icp` endpoint is publicly exposed: [5](#0-4) 

### Impact Explanation

Any buyer who transfers more ICP than the swap can accept (due to the global cap or per-participant cap being hit) will have their excess ICP locked in the swap canister's subaccount for the entire OPEN phase. If the swap is COMMITTED and `sweep_icp` is called before the buyer calls `error_refund_icp`, the accepted portion is swept to SNS governance, but the excess remains in the subaccount. Buyers who are unaware of the `error_refund_icp` mechanism may permanently lose the excess ICP. The integration test spec at line 140 confirms this is a known, expected behavior: [6](#0-5) 

The test `test_max_icp` demonstrates the partial acceptance: a buyer deposits 6 ICP but only 4 is accepted when the global cap is hit, with no automatic refund: [7](#0-6) 

**Impact**: Excess ICP is locked during the OPEN phase (duration can be days/weeks). After close, it is recoverable only via a manual `error_refund_icp` call. Buyers who do not know about this mechanism permanently lose the excess. This is a ledger conservation bug: ICP is transferred in, only part is credited as participation, and the "change" is not returned.

### Likelihood Explanation

This is reachable by any unprivileged user who calls `refresh_buyer_tokens` after transferring ICP to the swap canister. It is triggered whenever the swap's global ICP cap or per-participant cap is hit — a normal, expected condition in any popular SNS sale. The likelihood is **high** for popular swaps that fill up quickly, as the last participants to call `refresh_buyer_tokens` will routinely have partial deposits.

### Recommendation

In `refresh_buyer_token_e8s`, after computing `actual_increment_e8s`, if `actual_increment_e8s < requested_increment_e8s`, immediately attempt to transfer the excess ICP (`requested_increment_e8s - actual_increment_e8s`) back to the buyer's account before returning. This resolves the TODO at line 1133. If the refund transfer fails, the function should still succeed (the excess remains recoverable via `error_refund_icp`), but the attempt should be made automatically.

### Proof of Concept

1. A swap is configured with `max_direct_participation_icp_e8s = 10 * E8`.
2. User A deposits and calls `refresh_buyer_tokens` with 6 ICP — accepted in full.
3. User B transfers 6 ICP to their subaccount on the swap canister.
4. User B calls `refresh_buyer_tokens` — only 4 ICP is accepted (global cap hit), 2 ICP excess is locked.
5. During the OPEN phase, User B cannot recover the 2 ICP excess — `error_refund_icp` returns `"Error refunds can only be performed when the swap is ABORTED or COMMITTED"`.
6. The swap commits. `sweep_icp` transfers User B's 4 ICP to SNS governance.
7. User B must now know to call `error_refund_icp` to recover the 2 ICP excess. If they do not, the ICP is permanently stranded in the subaccount. [8](#0-7) [9](#0-8)
