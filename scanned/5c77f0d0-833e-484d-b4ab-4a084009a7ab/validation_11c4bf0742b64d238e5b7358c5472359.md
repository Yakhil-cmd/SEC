### Title
SNS Swap `refresh_buyer_token_e8s` Lacks Minimum Accepted Participation Guard, Locking Participant ICP Without Achieving Participation - (File: rs/sns/swap/src/swap.rs)

---

### Summary

The SNS swap canister's two-step participation flow (ICP ledger transfer → `refresh_buyer_tokens` call) has no slippage-protection parameter. A participant who transfers ICP to their swap subaccount can have that ICP locked for the entire remaining swap duration if another participant fills the remaining capacity between the transfer and the `refresh_buyer_tokens` call. The locked ICP is irrecoverable until the swap reaches `COMMITTED` or `ABORTED` state. The codebase itself acknowledges this with an open TODO.

---

### Finding Description

Participating in an SNS decentralization swap requires two sequential steps:

1. Transfer ICP to the buyer's personal subaccount on the swap canister (an irreversible ICP ledger operation).
2. Call `refresh_buyer_tokens` (which internally calls `refresh_buyer_token_e8s`) to register the participation.

`refresh_buyer_token_e8s` performs a pre-await check and a post-await check for whether the ICP target has been reached: [1](#0-0) 

Between the pre-await check and the post-await check, the function awaits a cross-canister call to the ICP ledger to read the subaccount balance: [2](#0-1) 

During this await point, another participant can call `refresh_buyer_tokens` and fill the remaining `available_direct_participation_e8s()`. When Alice's call resumes, the post-await `validate_possibility_of_direct_participation` check fails: [3](#0-2) 

Alice's call returns an error. Her ICP is now sitting in her swap subaccount but is **not registered** in `self.buyers`. She cannot call `refresh_buyer_tokens` again because the swap is full. She cannot call `error_refund_icp` because that endpoint is gated to `ABORTED` or `COMMITTED` lifecycle states only: [4](#0-3) 

The docstring on `refresh_buyer_token_e8s` explicitly acknowledges this lock and defers recovery to post-close: [5](#0-4) 

An open TODO confirms the issue has not been resolved: [6](#0-5) 

There is no `min_accepted_icp_e8s` parameter in `RefreshBuyerTokensRequest` that would allow a participant to specify a minimum acceptable participation amount and abort cleanly if that threshold cannot be met. [7](#0-6) 

---

### Impact Explanation

A participant who transfers ICP to the swap subaccount expecting to participate can have their ICP locked for the entire remaining duration of the swap — potentially days to weeks — without receiving any SNS tokens or governance neurons. The ICP is not permanently lost, but it is inaccessible until the swap closes. During this period the participant cannot use those funds for any other purpose. The swap's `swap_due_timestamp_seconds` parameter defines the maximum lock duration. [8](#0-7) 

---

### Likelihood Explanation

This is not a theoretical race condition. The IC canister execution model guarantees that any `await` point is a yield point where other messages can be interleaved. The ICP ledger balance query at line 1158–1163 is exactly such a yield point. Any other participant whose `refresh_buyer_tokens` message is scheduled during that window and whose contribution fills the remaining capacity will trigger this condition. No privileged access, no mempool observation, and no malicious intent is required — ordinary concurrent participation near the swap's capacity ceiling is sufficient. Swaps that are popular and near their `max_direct_participation_icp_e8s` cap are at highest risk. [9](#0-8) 

---

### Recommendation

Add a `min_accepted_icp_e8s: Option<u64>` field to `RefreshBuyerTokensRequest`. After computing `new_balance_e8s`, if the accepted increment is less than the caller-supplied minimum, return an error **before** writing any state. This gives participants the same slippage-protection guarantee that Uniswap v2 provides via `amountOutMin`. Additionally, resolve `TODO(NNS1-1682)` by attempting an immediate refund of unacceptable ICP within the same call rather than deferring to post-close `error_refund_icp`.

---

### Proof of Concept

```
Swap params: max_direct_participation_icp_e8s = 10 ICP, min_participant_icp_e8s = 1 ICP.
Current state: 9 ICP already committed by existing participants. 1 ICP remaining.

1. Alice observes 1 ICP remaining capacity.
2. Alice transfers 1 ICP to her swap subaccount on the ICP ledger (irreversible).
3. Alice calls refresh_buyer_tokens.
4. Pre-await check passes: available_direct_participation_e8s() == 1 ICP > 0.
5. Swap canister awaits ICP ledger account_balance call (yield point).
6. Bob's refresh_buyer_tokens message executes during the yield, committing the final 1 ICP.
   available_direct_participation_e8s() is now 0; icp_target_progress is Reached.
7. Alice's call resumes. Post-await validate_possibility_of_direct_participation() returns:
   Err("The ICP target for this token swap has already been reached.")
8. Alice's call returns an error. self.buyers is unchanged (Alice not registered).
9. Alice's 1 ICP sits in her swap subaccount.
10. Alice calls refresh_buyer_tokens again → pre-await check fails immediately (swap full).
11. Alice calls error_refund_icp → rejected:
    "Error refunds can only be performed when the swap is ABORTED or COMMITTED"
12. Alice's 1 ICP is locked until swap_due_timestamp_seconds elapses and the swap closes.
``` [10](#0-9) [11](#0-10)
