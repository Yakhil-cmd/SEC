### Title
Excess ICP Not Automatically Refunded When Participant Exceeds Per-Participant Cap - (`rs/sns/swap/src/swap.rs`)

---

### Summary

The SNS Swap canister's `refresh_buyer_token_e8s` function caps accepted ICP at `max_participant_icp_e8s` but does not refund the excess ICP that remains in the participant's subaccount. The excess is locked for the entire duration of the swap's OPEN state and requires a separate manual call to `error_refund_icp()` after finalization. This is a direct analog to the Router.sol bug where unused ETH is not refunded when a limit price is set.

---

### Finding Description

In `rs/sns/swap/src/swap.rs`, `refresh_buyer_token_e8s` reads the full ICP balance from the participant's subaccount on the ICP ledger, then caps the accepted amount at `max_participant_icp_e8s`:

```rust
let actual_increment_e8s = std::cmp::min(max_increment_e8s, requested_increment_e8s);
let new_balance_e8s = old_amount_icp_e8s.saturating_add(actual_increment_e8s);
// ...
let new_balance_e8s = std::cmp::min(new_balance_e8s, max_participant_icp_e8s);
// ...
self.buyers
    .entry(buyer.to_string())
    .or_insert_with(|| BuyerState::new(0))
    .set_amount_icp_e8s(new_balance_e8s);
``` [1](#0-0) 

Only `new_balance_e8s` (the capped amount) is recorded in the `buyers` map. The excess ICP — the difference between what was transferred to the subaccount and what was accepted — is silently left in the participant's subaccount of the swap canister. The developers acknowledge this with a TODO:

```rust
// TODO(NNS1-1682): attempt to refund ICP that cannot be accepted.
pub async fn refresh_buyer_token_e8s(
``` [2](#0-1) 

The excess ICP cannot be recovered during the OPEN lifecycle because `error_refund_icp` enforces a lifecycle precondition:

```rust
if !(self.lifecycle() == Lifecycle::Aborted || self.lifecycle() == Lifecycle::Committed) {
    return ErrorRefundIcpResponse::new_precondition_error(
        "Error refunds can only be performed when the swap is ABORTED or COMMITTED",
    );
}
``` [3](#0-2) 

After finalization, `sweep_icp` only transfers the recorded `amount_e8s` (the capped accepted amount), not the full subaccount balance:

```rust
let result = icp_transferable_amount
    .transfer_helper(
        now_fn,
        DEFAULT_TRANSFER_FEE,
        Some(subaccount),
        &dst,
        icp_ledger,
    )
    .await;
``` [4](#0-3) 

The excess ICP remains in the subaccount after `sweep_icp` and can only be recovered by the participant manually calling `error_refund_icp()`: [5](#0-4) 

---

### Impact Explanation

A participant who transfers more ICP than `max_participant_icp_e8s` to their swap subaccount has their excess ICP locked for the entire duration of the swap's OPEN state (which can last days or weeks). After finalization, the excess is not automatically returned — the participant must discover and manually invoke `error_refund_icp()`. If they do not, the ICP remains stranded in the subaccount indefinitely (though it is always recoverable by calling `error_refund_icp()` at any future time).

Unlike the Router.sol bug, the excess ICP cannot be extracted by a third party — `error_refund_icp` always transfers from `subaccount(swap_canister, source_principal_id)` to `source_principal_id`'s own account:

```rust
let dst = Account {
    owner: source_principal_id.0,
    subaccount: None,
};
``` [6](#0-5) 

The impact is therefore: **temporary but potentially extended lock-up of user funds with no automatic recovery path**, rather than outright theft. Severity is Medium.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

This is triggered by any participant who sends more ICP than `max_participant_icp_e8s` to their subaccount before calling `refresh_buyer_tokens`. This is a realistic scenario:

- A participant may not know the exact current cap (e.g., if the swap is near its `max_direct_participation_icp_e8s` ceiling, `available_direct_participation_e8s()` shrinks dynamically).
- A participant may intentionally over-send to ensure they hit the maximum.
- The ticketing system does not prevent over-sending at the ledger level; it only validates the ticket amount against the requested increment. [7](#0-6) 

---

### Recommendation

Inside `refresh_buyer_token_e8s`, after computing `new_balance_e8s` and recording it in the `buyers` map, immediately attempt to refund the excess ICP (`e8s - new_balance_e8s`) back to the participant's principal account via an ICP ledger transfer. This mirrors the recommended fix for the Router.sol analog. The TODO at line 1133 (`NNS1-1682`) already tracks this work. [8](#0-7) 

---

### Proof of Concept

**Scenario**: SNS swap with `max_participant_icp_e8s = 5 ICP`, `max_direct_participation_icp_e8s = 100 ICP`.

1. Alice transfers **6 ICP** to `subaccount(swap_canister, alice)` on the ICP ledger.
2. Alice calls `refresh_buyer_tokens`. The canister reads the balance as 6 ICP, caps it at 5 ICP, records `buyers[alice] = 5 ICP`. The remaining **1 ICP stays in the subaccount**.
3. Alice immediately calls `error_refund_icp(alice)` to recover the 1 ICP — it fails with `"Error refunds can only be performed when the swap is ABORTED or COMMITTED"`.
4. The swap runs for two weeks. Alice's 1 ICP is locked and unavailable.
5. The swap commits. `sweep_icp` transfers exactly 5 ICP from Alice's subaccount to SNS governance. The 1 ICP excess remains.
6. Alice must now remember to call `error_refund_icp(alice)` to recover the 1 ICP (minus the transfer fee). If she does not, the ICP remains stranded.

This is confirmed by the integration test comment:

> "Excess directly participated ICP (minus the fees) are refunded" — only via `Swap.error_refund_icp()`, not automatically. [9](#0-8)
