### Title
Excess ICP Sent Above Per-Participant Cap Is Not Refunded During OPEN State - (File: rs/sns/swap/src/swap.rs)

---

### Summary

In the SNS Swap canister's `refresh_buyer_token_e8s` function, when a buyer sends more ICP than the `max_participant_icp_e8s` or global `max_direct_participation_e8s` cap allows, only the capped amount is recorded in the buyer's state. The excess ICP remains locked in the buyer's subaccount of the swap canister for the entire OPEN state with no automatic refund. Recovery requires a separate `error_refund_icp` call only after the swap closes. This is a direct IC analog to the ERC20 leftover-token-not-refunded vulnerability class.

---

### Finding Description

`refresh_buyer_token_e8s` in `rs/sns/swap/src/swap.rs` caps the accepted ICP at two levels:

1. The global remaining capacity (`max_increment_e8s = self.available_direct_participation_e8s()`) at line 1224.
2. The per-participant cap (`max_participant_icp_e8s`) at line 1237.

---

### Impact Explanation

Any buyer who sends more ICP than the per-participant cap (`max_participant_icp_e8s`) or the remaining global capacity has their excess ICP locked in the swap canister's subaccount for the entire duration of the OPEN state. The excess is not automatically returned. After the swap closes (COMMITTED or ABORTED), the buyer must manually call `error_refund_icp` to recover the excess. This is a ledger conservation bug: tokens that should be returned to the user are not returned, and the user must take an additional step to recover them. If the user is unaware of `error_refund_icp`, the excess ICP may remain stranded in the swap canister indefinitely. [6](#0-5) 

---

### Likelihood Explanation

This is reachable by any unprivileged ingress sender participating in an SNS swap. It occurs whenever a buyer sends more ICP than the per-participant cap or the remaining global capacity. This can happen:
- Accidentally, because the exact remaining capacity is not known at the time of transfer (it can change between the transfer and the `refresh_buyer_tokens` call due to concurrent participants).
- Intentionally, when a buyer tries to maximize their participation.

The swap canister's own test suite (`test_max_icp`) demonstrates this scenario where the second buyer sends 6 ICP but only 4 ICP is accepted, with the remaining 2 ICP left in the subaccount. [7](#0-6) 

---

### Recommendation

Implement the acknowledged TODO at line 1133 (`TODO(NNS1-1682): attempt to refund ICP that cannot be accepted`). After computing the capped `new_balance_e8s`, if `e8s > new_balance_e8s`, the function should immediately initiate a transfer of the excess `(e8s - new_balance_e8s)` back to the buyer's account on the ICP ledger. This mirrors the pattern already used in `error_refund_icp` and eliminates the need for buyers to manually recover excess ICP after the swap closes. [8](#0-7) 

---

### Proof of Concept

1. An SNS swap is in OPEN state with `max_participant_icp_e8s = 5 * E8` and `max_direct_participation_icp_e8s = 10 * E8`.
2. Buyer A sends `6 * E8` ICP to their subaccount (`principal_to_subaccount(buyer_a)`) of the swap canister on the ICP ledger.
3. Buyer A calls `refresh_buyer_tokens`.
4. `refresh_buyer_token_e8s` reads the balance as `6 * E8`, caps it to `5 * E8`, records `5 * E8` in `buyer_state`, and returns `Ok(RefreshBuyerTokensResponse { icp_accepted_participation_e8s: 5*E8, icp_ledger_account_balance_e8s: 6*E8 })`.
5. The excess `1 * E8` ICP remains in the subaccount. No refund is issued.
6. Buyer A calls `error_refund_icp` — it is rejected with "ICP cannot be refunded as principal X has Y ICP (e8s) in escrow" because the swap is still OPEN.
7. The `1 * E8` excess is inaccessible until the swap closes (COMMITTED or ABORTED).
8. After the swap closes and `sweep_icp` runs (transferring only the recorded `5 * E8`), Buyer A must call `error_refund_icp` to recover the remaining `1 * E8 - fee`. [9](#0-8) [10](#0-9)
