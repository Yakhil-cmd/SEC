### Title
SNS Swap `refresh_buyer_tokens` Applies Confirmation-Text Consent Check to Caller Instead of Designated Buyer — (`rs/sns/swap/canister/canister.rs`)

---

### Summary

The SNS Swap canister's `refresh_buyer_tokens` endpoint accepts an optional `buyer` field that can name any principal other than the actual caller. The swap's confirmation-text gate — the only per-call consent mechanism — is validated against the **caller's** supplied text, while participation is recorded for the **buyer** principal. This mirrors the exact structural defect in the external report: an authorization/consent check is applied to the wrong identity (the caller instead of the beneficiary whose account is being credited).

---

The structural mismatch is:

| What is checked | What is recorded |
|---|---|
| Caller provides `confirmation_text` | `buyer` (third-party) is registered as participant |

The **buyer** never provides the confirmation text; the **caller** does. This is the same inversion as the Solidity bug: the gating check is applied to the wrong identity.

---

### Impact Explanation

1. **Consent bypass.** If an SNS configures a `confirmation_text` (a legally or operationally significant acknowledgment), a third-party caller can register any principal as a swap participant — including one that has deliberately not called `refresh_buyer_tokens` because they have not agreed to the terms — as long as the caller knows the text (which is public, set at SNS initialization).

2. **Forced participation.** A buyer who has pre-funded their subaccount (e.g., via the ticket flow) but has not yet decided to participate can be force-enrolled by any other principal. Once enrolled, the buyer's ICP is committed to the swap and can only be recovered after the swap closes (committed or aborted), via `error_refund_icp`.

3. **Confirmation-text gate is rendered meaningless for third-party calls.** The gate was designed so that each participant explicitly acknowledges the terms. When `buyer ≠ caller`, the gate is satisfied by the caller's knowledge of a public string, not by the buyer's consent.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

- The `buyer` field is documented and intentionally accepted; any unprivileged ingress sender can supply it.
- The confirmation text is set during SNS initialization and is publicly readable from swap state.
- The only prerequisite is that the target buyer has ICP sitting in their swap subaccount (e.g., after using the ticket flow but before calling `refresh_buyer_tokens` themselves).
- No privileged access, key material, or majority corruption is required.

---

### Recommendation

Inside `refresh_buyer_token_e8s`, or at the canister boundary in `refresh_buyer_tokens`, add a check that enforces: **if `buyer ≠ caller`, the confirmation text requirement is waived only if the SNS explicitly allows third-party notification (i.e., no confirmation text is configured)**. When a confirmation text is configured, either:

- Reject calls where `buyer ≠ caller`, or
- Record the **caller** as the consenting party and require the **buyer** to separately confirm before their participation is finalized.

This mirrors the recommended fix in the external report: the consent/authorization check must be applied to the party whose identity matters — the actual caller — not the beneficiary named in the request.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. SNS is initialized with `confirmation_text = "I agree to the terms"`.
2. Alice transfers 100 ICP to her swap subaccount (`swap_canister / principal_to_subaccount(Alice)`).
3. Alice has not called `refresh_buyer_tokens` because she has not agreed to the terms.
4. Bob (any unprivileged principal) calls:
   ```
   refresh_buyer_tokens({
     buyer: Alice.principal,
     confirmation_text: "I agree to the terms"
   })
   ```
5. The swap validates Bob's confirmation text (passes), reads Alice's subaccount balance (100 ICP), and inserts Alice into `self.buyers` with 100 ICP committed.
6. Alice is now a registered swap participant without ever having provided the confirmation text herself. Her ICP is locked until swap finalization. [1](#0-0) [6](#0-5)
