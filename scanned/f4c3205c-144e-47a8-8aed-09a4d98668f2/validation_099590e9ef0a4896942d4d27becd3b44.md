### Title
Excess ICP Sent to SNS Swap Subaccount Is Not Immediately Returned to the Buyer - (File: rs/sns/swap/src/swap.rs)

### Summary

The `refresh_buyer_token_e8s` function in the SNS Swap canister reads the full ICP balance from a buyer's subaccount but only records up to `available_direct_participation_e8s()` as accepted participation. Any excess ICP beyond the per-participant or total-swap cap remains locked in the buyer's subaccount on the ICP ledger for the entire duration of the swap's Open state. The excess can only be recovered by calling `error_refund_icp` after the swap reaches a terminal state (Committed or Aborted) and after the sweep completes — a multi-step process the user must actively initiate.

### Finding Description

`refresh_buyer_token_e8s` is the second step of the SNS participation flow: the buyer first transfers ICP to their dedicated subaccount of the Swap canister on the ICP ledger, then calls this function to register the participation. [1](#0-0) 

The function queries the full subaccount balance: [2](#0-1) 

It then computes the maximum additional ICP that can be accepted given the remaining swap capacity: [3](#0-2) 

Only up to `max_increment_e8s` is credited to the buyer's state. Any ICP in the subaccount beyond that cap is silently left behind — the function neither transfers it back nor records it for later automatic refund. The existing TODO comment acknowledges this gap: [4](#0-3) 

The only recovery path is `error_refund_icp`, which is gated behind two sequential preconditions:

1. The swap lifecycle must be `Aborted` or `Committed`: [5](#0-4) 

2. If the buyer participated and the swap committed, the buyer's ICP must already have been swept (i.e., `transfer_success_timestamp_seconds != 0`): [6](#0-5) 

Only after both conditions are satisfied can the buyer recover the excess: [7](#0-6) 

### Impact Explanation

A buyer who sends more ICP than the swap can accept (e.g., because the swap is nearly full, or because the per-participant maximum is lower than the amount sent) has their excess ICP locked in the Swap canister's subaccount for the entire remaining Open period of the swap. They cannot use those funds elsewhere. If the swap commits, they must additionally wait for `sweep_icp` to complete before `error_refund_icp` becomes callable. If the buyer is unaware of the `error_refund_icp` mechanism, the excess ICP may remain stranded indefinitely. This is a **ledger conservation bug**: ICP sent by the user is not fully accounted for within the swap's accepted participation, and the surplus is not proactively returned.

### Likelihood Explanation

The scenario is reachable by any unprivileged ingress sender. It is triggered whenever a buyer sends more ICP than `available_direct_participation_e8s()` allows — a common race condition when multiple buyers participate near the swap's ICP cap. The two-step participation flow (transfer then notify) makes over-sending likely in practice, since the available capacity can change between the transfer and the `refresh_buyer_tokens` call.

### Recommendation

After computing `max_increment_e8s` and determining the accepted amount, if the subaccount balance exceeds the accepted amount, immediately initiate a transfer back to the buyer's principal account for the excess (minus the ICP ledger transfer fee). This mirrors the fix applied to the analogous Solidity vulnerability: return excess payment to the sender within the same transaction/call. The existing TODO at line 1133 (`TODO(NNS1-1682): attempt to refund ICP that cannot be accepted`) already identifies this as a known gap.

### Proof of Concept

1. A swap is open with `max_direct_participation_icp_e8s = 100 ICP` and `max_participant_icp_e8s = 40 ICP`. Buyers A and B have each already contributed 40 ICP (total: 80 ICP). Only 20 ICP of capacity remains.
2. Buyer C transfers 40 ICP to their subaccount of the Swap canister.
3. Buyer C calls `refresh_buyer_tokens`. The function reads the 40 ICP balance but `available_direct_participation_e8s()` returns 20 ICP. Only 20 ICP is credited to Buyer C's state; the remaining 20 ICP stays in the subaccount.
4. Buyer C calls `error_refund_icp` immediately — it fails with `"Error refunds can only be performed when the swap is ABORTED or COMMITTED"`.
5. The swap runs for its full duration (potentially weeks). Buyer C's 20 ICP is locked and unavailable.
6. After the swap commits and `sweep_icp` runs, Buyer C can finally call `error_refund_icp` to recover the 20 ICP minus the ICP ledger transfer fee.

This matches the test scenario documented in the codebase: [8](#0-7)
