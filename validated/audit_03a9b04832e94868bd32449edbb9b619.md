### Title
`ExitToNear` Precompile Uses `ft_transfer` Instead of `ft_transfer_call` in Legacy Mode, Bypassing Recipient Contract Callbacks — (File: `engine-precompiles/src/native.rs`)

---

### Summary

The `ExitToNear` precompile selects `"ft_transfer"` (not `"ft_transfer_call"`) when bridging base-ETH or ERC-20 tokens from Aurora EVM to NEAR in the default/legacy code path. This is the direct NEP-141 analog of using ERC-721 `transferFrom` instead of `safeTransferFrom`: the recipient NEAR contract's `ft_on_transfer` callback is never invoked. Any NEAR smart contract that relies on `ft_on_transfer` to register an incoming deposit will silently receive tokens into its NEP-141 balance while its internal accounting remains unupdated, permanently freezing the bridged funds from the user's perspective.

---

### Finding Description

In `engine-precompiles/src/native.rs`, two functions build the outbound NEAR promise for the `ExitToNear` precompile.

**`exit_base_token_to_near`** — base-ETH bridge path:

When `exit_params.message` is `None` (no `:` suffix in the recipient string), the function returns the literal string `"ft_transfer"` as the NEAR method to call: [1](#0-0) 

**`exit_erc20_token_to_near`** — ERC-20 bridge path:

The catch-all `_` arm (which handles both `None` and any unrecognised suffix) also returns `"ft_transfer"`: [2](#0-1) 

The selected method string is then used verbatim to construct the outbound NEAR promise: [3](#0-2) 

`ft_transfer` is a simple NEP-141 balance move. It does **not** invoke `ft_on_transfer` on the recipient. `ft_transfer_call` is the NEP-141 equivalent of ERC-721 `safeTransferFrom`: it transfers the tokens **and** calls `ft_on_transfer` on the recipient contract, allowing it to register the deposit, update internal accounting, or reject the transfer.

The Omni path already uses `ft_transfer_call` correctly (lines 533 and 614), confirming the engine is aware of the distinction. The legacy path does not.

---

### Impact Explanation

**Permanent freezing of funds (Critical).**

The sequence for a victim user:

1. User holds an ERC-20 token on Aurora (e.g., a bridged USDC mirror).
2. User calls the `ExitToNear` precompile with flag `0x01` and a recipient that is a NEAR DeFi contract (vault, lending protocol, DEX router) — without appending a `:` message, which is the natural default.
3. The ERC-20 contract burns the user's tokens on the Aurora side (irreversible).
4. The precompile schedules `ft_transfer` on the NEP-141 contract, crediting the NEAR DeFi contract's NEP-141 balance.
5. The NEAR DeFi contract's `ft_on_transfer` is **never called**; its internal deposit ledger is never updated.
6. The user's tokens sit in the contract's raw NEP-141 balance, inaccessible through any of the contract's intended user-facing methods.
7. The user has no recourse: the Aurora-side burn is final, and the NEAR-side contract has no mechanism to credit a deposit that arrived without `ft_on_transfer`.

This matches the Critical impact tier: **permanent freezing of user funds**.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**Medium.** The `ExitToNear` precompile is a primary, publicly documented bridge exit path reachable by any EVM user. The legacy (no-message) input format is the simplest and most natural invocation. NEAR DeFi protocols (vaults, AMMs, lending markets) universally use `ft_transfer_call` / `ft_on_transfer` as their deposit entry point — this is the NEP-141 standard pattern. A user bridging tokens to interact with such a protocol will naturally omit the message suffix, triggering the vulnerable path. No special privileges or attacker cooperation are required.

---

### Recommendation

Replace `"ft_transfer"` with `"ft_transfer_call"` in both legacy branches, supplying an empty `msg` field. This ensures the recipient contract's `ft_on_transfer` is always invoked, mirroring the behaviour of ERC-721 `safeTransferFrom`. The `CallyNft` fix in the original report (inheriting `ERC721TokenReceiver`) has a direct analog here: the Aurora engine contract itself should implement `ft_on_transfer` to handle any refunded amounts that `ft_transfer_call` may return.

Concretely, in `exit_base_token_to_near` and `exit_erc20_token_to_near`, change the legacy arm to produce `ft_transfer_call` args with `"msg": ""` and return `"ft_transfer_call"` as the method string, consistent with the Omni path already present in both functions.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Deploy a NEAR contract `vault.near` that implements `ft_on_transfer` to credit deposits and has no other deposit entry point.
2. From an Aurora EVM account, hold 100 units of a bridged ERC-20 (e.g., `usdc.aurora`).
3. Call the `ExitToNear` precompile at `0xe9217bc70b7ed1f598ddd3199e80b093fa71124f` with:
   - `flag = 0x01` (ERC-20 exit)
   - `amount = 100`
   - `recipient_account_id = "vault.near"` (no `:` suffix → legacy path)
4. Observe: the ERC-20 burn succeeds on Aurora; `ft_transfer` is called on the NEP-141 contract; `vault.near`'s NEP-141 balance increases by 100.
5. Observe: `vault.near`'s `ft_on_transfer` is **never called**; the user's deposit is not recorded in the vault's internal ledger.
6. The user's 100 tokens are permanently inaccessible — burned on Aurora, stranded in `vault.near`'s raw balance.
