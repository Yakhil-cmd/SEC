### Title
EIP-7623 Floor Gas Not Applied to Refund Calculation, Allowing Underpayment of Calldata Fees — (`engine/src/engine.rs`)

### Summary

Aurora Engine correctly validates that a transaction's `gas_limit` meets the EIP-7623 floor (`max(intrinsic_gas, floor_gas)`), but it does not apply the floor when computing the gas used for the refund. As a result, any user submitting a transaction with large calldata but low execution cost pays only the EVM execution gas rather than the EIP-7623 floor, receiving a larger refund than the protocol intends. Relayers lose the corresponding priority-fee income, and the network's calldata-cost floor is bypassed.

---

### Finding Description

`apply_transaction` in `engine/src/engine.rs` computes both `intrinsic_gas` and `floor_gas` and correctly rejects transactions whose `gas_limit` is below `max(intrinsic_gas, floor_gas)`: [1](#0-0) 

After EVM execution, however, `gas_used` is taken directly from the executor result without applying the floor: [2](#0-1) 

`floor_gas` is never referenced again after the limit check. The `refund_unused_gas` call therefore computes the refund as `prepaid_amount − execution_gas_used` instead of the EIP-7623-correct `prepaid_amount − max(execution_gas_used, floor_gas)`.

The floor-gas formula itself is implemented correctly in `NormalizedEthTransaction::floor_gas`: [3](#0-2) 

For a Prague-config transaction with 1 000 non-zero calldata bytes the floor is `4 000 × 10 + 21 000 = 61 000` gas. If the EVM execution only consumes 21 000 gas (e.g., a plain ETH transfer), the user is refunded `gas_limit − 21 000` instead of `gas_limit − 61 000`, paying 40 000 gas fewer fees than EIP-7623 requires.

---

### Impact Explanation

**High — Theft of unclaimed yield (relayer fee income).**

The relayer's priority-fee income is `gas_used × priority_fee_per_gas`. Because `gas_used` is understated by up to `floor_gas − execution_gas_used`, the relayer receives less than the protocol intends. The difference is returned to the sender as an excess refund. For a 1 000-byte non-zero calldata transaction at a 1 Gwei priority fee the shortfall is `40 000 × 1e9 = 40 µETH` per transaction; at scale this is a sustained drain on relayer revenue. Additionally, users can consume NEAR gas resources (the actual cost of processing calldata on the host chain) for less Aurora ETH than the floor is designed to enforce, enabling a resource-exhaustion vector.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**High.** No special privilege is required. Any Aurora user can craft a transaction with large calldata and a minimal-execution target (e.g., a plain ETH transfer to an EOA, or a call to a contract that returns immediately). The `submit` entry point is publicly reachable. The condition `floor_gas > execution_gas_used` is trivially satisfied whenever calldata is non-trivial and execution is cheap.

---

### Recommendation

After obtaining `gas_used` from the executor result, clamp it to the floor before passing it to `refund_unused_gas`:

```rust
let gas_used = match &result {
    Ok(submit_result) => submit_result.gas_used,
    Err(engine_err)   => engine_err.gas_used,
};
// EIP-7623: total gas used is max(execution_gas_used, floor_gas)
let gas_used = gas_used.max(floor_gas);
```

This mirrors the fix applied to Optimism's `CrossDomainMessenger.baseGas()` and ensures the refund is computed against the correct floor.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Construct a Prague-config EVM transaction:
   - `data`: 1 000 non-zero bytes (e.g., `vec![0xAB; 1000]`)
   - `to`: any EOA (plain transfer, execution cost = 21 000 gas)
   - `gas_limit`: 70 000 (satisfies `max(intrinsic_gas=37000, floor_gas=61000)`)
   - `gas_price` / `priority_fee`: 1 Gwei

2. Submit via `submit`. The engine:
   - Charges the sender `70 000 × 1 Gwei` upfront. ✓
   - Executes the transfer; EVM reports `gas_used = 21 000`.
   - Refunds `(70 000 − 21 000) × 1 Gwei = 49 000 Gwei` to the sender.

3. Expected under EIP-7623:
   - `floor_gas = (1000 × 4) × 10 + 21 000 = 61 000`
   - Correct refund: `(70 000 − 61 000) × 1 Gwei = 9 000 Gwei`
   - Relayer shortfall: `40 000 Gwei` per transaction.

The sender retains 40 000 Gwei more than the protocol intends; the relayer loses the same amount. Repeating this at volume constitutes a sustained theft of relayer yield and a bypass of the calldata-cost floor.

    #[allow(clippy::naive_bytecount)]
    pub fn floor_gas(&self, config: &aurora_evm::Config) -> Result<u64, Error> {
        if config.has_floor_gas {
            let num_zero_bytes = u64::try_from(self.data.iter().filter(|b| **b == 0).count())
                .map_err(|_e| Error::IntegerConversion)?;
            let data_len = u64::try_from(self.data.len()).map_err(|_e| Error::IntegerConversion)?;
            let num_non_zero_bytes = data_len
                .checked_sub(num_zero_bytes)
                .ok_or(Error::GasOverflow)?;

            let base_gas = config.gas_transaction_call;
            let tokens_in_calldata = num_non_zero_bytes
                .checked_mul(4)
                .and_then(|gas| gas.checked_add(num_zero_bytes))
                .ok_or(Error::GasOverflow)?;

            tokens_in_calldata
                .checked_mul(config.total_cost_floor_per_token)
                .and_then(|gas| gas.checked_add(base_gas))
                .ok_or(Error::GasOverflow)
        } else {
            Ok(0)
        }
    }
