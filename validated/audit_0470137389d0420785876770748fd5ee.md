### Title
Proportional Speed-Limit Catch-Up in `compute_maturity_modulation_permyriad` Allows Large Modulation Jumps After Multi-Day XRC Outage, Causing Unfair ICP Reduction for Pending Disbursements - (File: `rs/nns/governance/src/timer_tasks/update_icp_xdr_rate_related_data.rs`)

---

### Summary

When the Exchange Rate Canister (XRC) is unavailable for multiple consecutive days, the NNS Governance maturity modulation is frozen at its last computed value. When XRC becomes available again, `compute_maturity_modulation_permyriad` applies a **proportional speed limit** (`N × 30 permyriad` for `N` missed days), allowing the modulation to jump by up to the full range (−1000 to +200 permyriad) in a single update. Neuron owners with pending maturity disbursements (7-day non-cancellable delay) have no grace period to react before this large modulation swing is applied at finalization, causing them to receive significantly less ICP than expected.

---

### Finding Description

The `UpdateIcpXdrRateRelatedData` recurring task fetches daily ICP/XDR rates from XRC and computes a `MaturityModulation` value stored in `heap_data.maturity_modulation`. This value is applied when neurons spawn or when maturity disbursements are finalized.

**Root cause — proportional catch-up with no grace period:**

In `compute_maturity_modulation_permyriad`, when the timer detects that multiple days have elapsed since the last update, it intentionally widens the allowed daily change: [1](#0-0) 

Specifically, `max_change = MATURITY_MODULATION_DAILY_SPEED_LIMIT_PERMYRIAD.saturating_mul(days_elapsed as i64)` = `30 × N` permyriad for `N` missed days. With `N = 34`, `max_change = 1020 permyriad`, which spans nearly the entire allowed range of [−1000, +200] permyriad.

**When does `days_elapsed > 1` occur?**

The `updated_at_days_since_epoch` field is only written when `compute_maturity_modulation_permyriad` succeeds. If XRC returns errors for multiple consecutive days, the modulation is left unchanged and `updated_at_days_since_epoch` grows stale: [2](#0-1) 

When XRC finally recovers, the next successful computation uses the stale `previous_day`, producing a large `days_elapsed` and a proportionally large allowed jump.

**No staleness check at disbursement finalization:**

Neither `maybe_spawn_neurons` nor `try_finalize_maturity_disbursement` checks how old the modulation is before applying it: [3](#0-2) [4](#0-3) 

**No cancellation mechanism for pending disbursements:**

Once `initiate_maturity_disbursement` is called, the maturity is deducted from the neuron and a `MaturityDisbursement` record is created with a fixed `finalize_disbursement_timestamp_seconds`. There is no cancel path: [5](#0-4) 

The disbursement delay is 7 days. During an XRC outage, users can still initiate disbursements while the modulation appears stable. When XRC recovers and the modulation jumps, those pending disbursements are finalized with the new (potentially very different) value.

**Documented range vs. actual range mismatch:**

The proto comment states the disbursement amount "can make the amount [95%, 105%] of the original amount," but the Mission 70 bounds are [−1000, +200] permyriad = [−10%, +2%]: [6](#0-5) [7](#0-6) 

---

### Impact Explanation

A neuron owner who initiates a maturity disbursement while the modulation is at +200 permyriad (ICP price above long-term average) and XRC is silently failing could, after a 34-day XRC outage, have their disbursement finalized at −820 permyriad — receiving 91.8% of expected ICP instead of 102%. For large maturity amounts (e.g., 10,000 ICP equivalent), this represents a loss of ~1,000 ICP with no recourse. The user had no opportunity to cancel the disbursement during the 7-day window because the modulation appeared stable throughout.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The XRC is an on-chain canister. Realistic causes of multi-day XRC unavailability include:
- XRC subnet halt (has occurred historically on the IC)
- XRC canister bug causing persistent errors
- Governance-controlled XRC upgrade that introduces a regression

A 7-day outage (matching the disbursement delay) allows a 210 permyriad jump. A 34-day outage allows a near-full-range jump. XRC outages of several days are plausible without any privileged attacker involvement. No external oracle, DNS hijack, or threshold attack is required — only the XRC canister being unreachable for an extended period.

---

### Recommendation

1. **Add a staleness guard before applying modulation.** In `maybe_spawn_neurons` and `try_finalize_maturity_disbursement`, check that `updated_at_days_since_epoch` is within an acceptable window (e.g., ≤ 2 days old) before applying the modulation. If stale, defer finalization rather than applying a potentially large jump.

2. **Introduce a grace period after modulation resumes.** After a multi-day gap is detected (`days_elapsed > 1`), delay finalization of pending disbursements by a configurable grace period (e.g., 24–48 hours) to allow neuron owners to observe the new modulation value before it is applied.

3. **Cap the single-step jump regardless of missed days.** Instead of `30 × N`, cap the maximum single-step change at `30 permyriad` regardless of `days_elapsed`, and allow the modulation to catch up gradually over subsequent daily updates.

---

### Proof of Concept

**Setup:** Modulation is at +200 permyriad (maximum). A neuron owner initiates a maturity disbursement of 10,000 ICP-equivalent maturity. The 7-day finalization window begins.

**Day 1–34:** XRC is unavailable. The `UpdateIcpXdrRateRelatedData` task fires daily but fails at `fetch_and_validate_rate`. The modulation stays at +200 permyriad. `updated_at_days_since_epoch` is not updated. [8](#0-7) 

**Day 35:** XRC recovers. The task fetches a valid rate reflecting a significant ICP price drop. `update_maturity_modulation` is called with `current_day = D+35`, `previous_day = D`. `days_elapsed = 35`, `max_change = 35 × 30 = 1050 permyriad`. Target modulation = −1000 (price crashed). New modulation = `clamp(−1000, 200 − 1050, 200 + 1050)` = `clamp(−1000, −850, 1250)` = **−850 permyriad**. [9](#0-8) 

**Finalization:** The neuron owner's disbursement (initiated on Day 1, due on Day 8) was already finalized at Day 8 with the then-current modulation. But if the disbursement was initiated on Day 28 (due Day 35), it is finalized on Day 35 with −850 permyriad. The owner receives `10,000 × (1 + (−850/10,000))` = **9,150 ICP** instead of the expected `10,200 ICP` — a loss of 1,050 ICP with no grace period and no cancellation option. [10](#0-9)
