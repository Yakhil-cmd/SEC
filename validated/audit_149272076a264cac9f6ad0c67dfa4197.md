### Title
SNS Governance `ManageLedgerParameters` Allows Transfer Fee Change During Active Decentralization Swap — (File: `rs/sns/governance/src/governance.rs`)

### Summary
The SNS governance canister's `perform_manage_ledger_parameters` function lacks a check for whether a decentralization swap is currently active. The SNS developer team, who control the initial developer neurons and hold governance majority during the swap window, can change the SNS ledger transfer fee mid-swap, retroactively increasing operational costs for all neurons received by swap participants — analogous to the auction platform owner changing the fee after the auction has started.

### Finding Description
In `rs/sns/governance/src/governance.rs`, `perform_manage_ledger_parameters` executes a `ManageLedgerParameters` proposal that changes the SNS ledger's transfer fee. The function calls `check_no_upgrades_in_progress` to guard against concurrent upgrades, but there is **no check for whether a decentralization swap is currently in `LIFECYCLE_OPEN` or `LIFECYCLE_ADOPTED` state**. [1](#0-0) 

When the proposal executes, it:
1. Upgrades the SNS ledger with the new `transfer_fee` via `upgrade_non_root_canister`
2. Unconditionally overwrites `NervousSystemParameters.transaction_fee_e8s` with the new value [2](#0-1) 

The `transaction_fee_e8s` stored in `NervousSystemParameters` is the fee enforced at execution time in `split_neuron` and `disburse_to_neuron`, both of which require `amount >= neuron_minimum_stake_e8s + transaction_fee_e8s`. [3](#0-2) [4](#0-3) 

The same attack surface exists via `ManageNervousSystemParameters`, which can change `transaction_fee_e8s` directly in governance parameters without a ledger upgrade. [5](#0-4) 

The SNS swap `Params` are immutable once set, but the fee governing neuron operations is not: [6](#0-5) 

### Impact Explanation
Swap participants who contributed ICP expecting a specific fee structure will find their neurons have materially higher operational costs after finalization. If `transaction_fee_e8s` is raised to approach `neuron_minimum_stake_e8s`, neurons from minimum-ICP contributions become effectively unusable: `split_neuron` and `disburse_to_neuron` will reject operations because the fee consumes the available stake margin. This is the direct IC analog of the auction platform owner setting the fee to 100% — participants cannot recover their staked tokens at the terms they agreed to. [7](#0-6) 

### Likelihood Explanation
Medium-Low. The SNS developer team controls the initial developer neurons and holds governance majority during the entire swap window (before swap tokens are distributed to participants). They can pass a `ManageLedgerParameters` proposal unilaterally during this window. The swap window can last days to weeks, and the developer team has a direct financial incentive to maximize their relative token allocation. The attack requires no external access — only submitting and voting on a governance proposal through the normal SNS governance interface.

### Recommendation
Add a lifecycle check in `perform_manage_ledger_parameters` (and in the `ManageNervousSystemParameters` handler for `transaction_fee_e8s` changes) that queries the SNS swap canister's current lifecycle state and rejects the proposal if the swap is in `LIFECYCLE_OPEN` or `LIFECYCLE_ADOPTED` state. The swap canister ID is available in `self.proto` at execution time. [8](#0-7) 

### Proof of Concept
1. SNS is created; developer team controls initial developer neurons with governance majority
2. Swap opens (`LIFECYCLE_OPEN`); participants contribute ICP at the published fee of 10,000 e8s
3. Developer neurons submit and pass a `ManageLedgerParameters` proposal setting `transfer_fee = 1_000_000` e8s (0.01 SNS tokens)
4. `perform_manage_ledger_parameters` executes without checking swap lifecycle state
5. SNS ledger is upgraded; `NervousSystemParameters.transaction_fee_e8s` is overwritten to `1_000_000`
6. Swap finalizes; neurons are created for participants with stakes calculated at the original price
7. Participants attempt `split_neuron` or `disburse_to_neuron` and find the fee (1,000,000 e8s) consumes the entire available margin above `neuron_minimum_stake_e8s`
8. Neurons from minimum-ICP contributions are permanently locked — neither splittable nor disbursable — at terms participants never agreed to [9](#0-8)
