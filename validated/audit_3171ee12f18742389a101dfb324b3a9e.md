### Title
SNS Swap `refresh_buyer_token_e8s` Freezes Excess ICP on Partial Participation Acceptance — (File: `rs/sns/swap/src/swap.rs`)

---

### Summary

When a buyer transfers more ICP to the SNS swap subaccount than the swap can accept (because `max_direct_participation_icp_e8s` is nearly exhausted), `refresh_buyer_token_e8s` records only the accepted portion in `BuyerState` but does not return the excess ICP to the buyer. The excess ICP remains locked in the buyer's swap subaccount for the entire open period of the swap (up to 90 days), with no mechanism to retrieve it until the swap closes. The code itself acknowledges this with an open TODO.

---

### Finding Description

In `rs/sns/swap/src/swap.rs`, `refresh_buyer_token_e8s` reads the buyer's subaccount balance `e8s` from the ICP ledger, then computes:

```
let max_increment_e8s = self.available_direct_participation_e8s();
let actual_increment_e8s = std::cmp::min(max_increment_e8s, requested_increment_e8s);
let new_balance_e8s = old_amount_icp_e8s.saturating_add(actual_increment_e8s);
```

Only `new_balance_e8s` is recorded in `BuyerState`. The difference `e8s - new_balance_e8s` — the excess ICP the buyer transferred but the swap cannot accept — remains sitting in the buyer's swap subaccount on the ICP ledger. No automatic refund is issued.

The code explicitly acknowledges this gap with a TODO comment immediately above the function:

```rust
// TODO(NNS1-1682): attempt to refund ICP that cannot be accepted.
```

The only recovery path is `error_refund_icp`, which is gated behind the swap being in `Committed` or `Aborted` lifecycle state. During the `Open` state the excess ICP is completely inaccessible to the buyer. [1](#0-0) [2](#0-1) [3](#0-2) 

The `sweep_icp` function, called during finalization, only transfers `amount_e8s` (the accepted amount) from each buyer's subaccount. The excess ICP that was transferred but not accepted is not swept, leaving it stranded until `error_refund_icp` is manually invoked post-close. [4](#0-3) 

The canister-level entry point is `refresh_buyer_tokens` in `rs/sns/swap/canister/canister.rs`, callable by any unprivileged principal. [5](#0-4) 

---

### Impact Explanation

A buyer who transfers 10 ICP when only 4 ICP of swap capacity remains will have 6 ICP locked in the swap subaccount for the entire open period. The buyer cannot use those funds elsewhere, cannot retrieve them, and receives no notification that the excess was not accepted. If the swap runs for its maximum duration (up to 90 days), the funds are inaccessible for that entire window. The `RefreshBuyerTokensResponse` does return `icp_accepted_participation_e8s` vs `icp_ledger_account_balance_e8s`, so the discrepancy is observable, but no automatic remediation occurs. [6](#0-5) 

---

### Likelihood Explanation

This is a realistic and common scenario. SNS swaps frequently fill up quickly; users who compute their transfer amount based on a stale view of `available_direct_participation_e8s` (a query call that can be outdated by the time the ICP transfer and `refresh_buyer_tokens` call complete) will routinely transfer more than the remaining capacity. The test `test_max_icp` in `rs/sns/swap/tests/swap.rs` explicitly demonstrates the scenario: TEST_USER2 deposits 6 ICP, only 4 ICP is accepted, and the remaining 2 ICP is silently left in the subaccount. [7](#0-6) 

---

### Recommendation

Implement the acknowledged TODO (NNS1-1682): after computing `actual_increment_e8s`, if `actual_increment_e8s < requested_increment_e8s`, immediately attempt an ICP ledger transfer to return the excess (`e8s - new_balance_e8s`, minus the transfer fee) back to the buyer's default account. If the refund transfer fails, log the failure and allow the buyer to recover via `error_refund_icp` post-close as a fallback.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. SNS swap is open with `max_direct_participation_icp_e8s = 10 ICP` and 6 ICP already committed.
2. Buyer transfers 6 ICP to their swap subaccount on the ICP ledger.
3. Buyer calls `refresh_buyer_tokens`.
4. `available_direct_participation_e8s()` returns 4 ICP.
5. `actual_increment_e8s = min(4, 6) = 4`; `BuyerState` records 4 ICP.
6. The remaining 2 ICP stays in the buyer's swap subaccount.
7. Buyer cannot call `error_refund_icp` — it rejects with "Error refunds can only be performed when the swap is ABORTED or COMMITTED".
8. The 2 ICP is inaccessible for the remainder of the swap's open period. [8](#0-7)
