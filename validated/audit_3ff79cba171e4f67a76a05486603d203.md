### Title
Hardcoded `EXIT_TO_NEAR_CALLBACK_GAS` Is Insufficient for the ERC-20 Refund Path in `exit_to_near_precompile_callback`, Causing Permanent Token Loss - (File: engine-precompiles/src/native.rs)

---

### Summary

When a user exits ERC-20 tokens to NEAR via the `exitToNear` precompile and the downstream `ft_transfer` promise fails (e.g., recipient not registered with the NEP-141 contract), the engine schedules `exit_to_near_precompile_callback` with a hardcoded `EXIT_TO_NEAR_CALLBACK_GAS = 10 TGas`. This callback must execute a full EVM call (`refund_on_error`) to re-mint the burned ERC-20 tokens. 10 TGas is at or below the minimum required for a non-trivial ERC-20 mint, meaning the callback can run out of NEAR gas, silently skip the refund, and permanently destroy the user's tokens.

---

### Finding Description

The `ExitToNear` precompile in `engine-precompiles/src/native.rs` schedules a two-step NEAR promise when the `error_refund` feature is compiled in:

1. **Base promise**: `ft_transfer` (or `ft_transfer_call`) on the NEP-141 contract, with `FT_TRANSFER_GAS = 10 TGas` or `FT_TRANSFER_CALL_GAS = 70 TGas`.
2. **Callback**: `exit_to_near_precompile_callback` on the Aurora engine account, with a hardcoded `EXIT_TO_NEAR_CALLBACK_GAS = 10 TGas`. [1](#0-0) 

The callback is always scheduled when `error_refund` is enabled because `refund_call_args` always returns `Some`: [2](#0-1) 

Inside `exit_to_near_precompile_callback`, when the base promise result is **not** `Successful`, the code calls `engine::refund_on_error`: [3](#0-2) 

`refund_on_error` runs a full EVM execution — for ERC-20 exits it calls `engine.call()` with `gas_limit = u64::MAX` to invoke the ERC-20 `mint` selector: [4](#0-3) 

The EVM-to-NEAR gas conversion ratio is approximately 175 MGas per EVM gas unit. A standard ERC-20 `mint` costs ~50,000 EVM gas ≈ 8.75 TGas, plus WASM overhead and storage I/O. The total easily reaches or exceeds 10 TGas for any non-trivial ERC-20 implementation. When the callback exhausts its 10 TGas budget, NEAR silently marks it as failed and the refund is never executed — the tokens burned in the EVM are gone permanently.

The constant itself carries no safety margin and was noted as experimentally determined: [5](#0-4) 

---

### Impact Explanation

**Impact: Critical — Permanent freezing/destruction of user funds.**

A user who exits ERC-20 tokens to a NEAR account that is not registered with the NEP-141 contract (a common mistake) will have their ERC-20 tokens burned in the EVM. The `ft_transfer` promise fails. The callback is invoked with 10 TGas, which is insufficient to re-mint the tokens via `refund_on_error`. The callback silently OOGs. The user's tokens are permanently destroyed with no recovery path.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**Likelihood: Low.**

- Requires the `error_refund` feature to be compiled into the production binary.
- Requires the `ft_transfer` base promise to fail (e.g., unregistered recipient, paused contract).
- Requires the ERC-20 contract's `mint` path to consume enough gas to exhaust 10 TGas in the callback context.

All three conditions are realistic in production: users regularly exit to unregistered accounts, and many bridged ERC-20 tokens have non-trivial mint logic.

---

### Recommendation

1. **Increase `EXIT_TO_NEAR_CALLBACK_GAS`** to a value that accounts for the worst-case EVM execution in `refund_on_error`. Based on the conversion ratio, a value of at least 30–50 TGas is appropriate.
2. **Alternatively**, split the refund into a separate, independently scheduled promise rather than bundling it into the same callback, so it receives its own gas budget.
3. **Add a gas-sufficiency check** at the start of `exit_to_near_precompile_callback`: if `env.prepaid_gas()` is below a threshold required for the refund path, return an error rather than silently OOGing.

---

### Proof of Concept

**Step 1**: User holds ERC-20 tokens on Aurora that are bridged from a NEP-141 contract.

**Step 2**: User calls the ERC-20 `burn`-and-exit function, which triggers the `exitToNear` precompile with a recipient NEAR account that is not registered with the NEP-141 contract.

**Step 3**: The precompile schedules:
- `ft_transfer` on the NEP-141 contract (10 TGas)
- `exit_to_near_precompile_callback` on Aurora (10 TGas) [6](#0-5) 

**Step 4**: `ft_transfer` fails because the recipient is unregistered. NEAR marks the base promise as `Failed`.

**Step 5**: `exit_to_near_precompile_callback` is invoked with 10 TGas. It reads the `refund` args and calls `engine::refund_on_error`, which runs the EVM to call `mint` on the ERC-20 contract. [7](#0-6) 

**Step 6**: The EVM execution for `mint` consumes ~8.75 TGas plus WASM/storage overhead, exhausting the 10 TGas budget. The NEAR runtime marks the callback as failed due to OOG.

**Step 7**: No refund is issued. The user's ERC-20 tokens, which were burned in Step 2, are permanently destroyed.
