### Title
Zero EVM Gas Cost for Exit Precompiles Enables Cheap Triggering of Expensive NEAR Cross-Contract Calls - (File: engine-precompiles/src/native.rs)

### Summary
The `ExitToNear` and `ExitToEthereum` precompiles charge **zero EVM gas** for their execution, yet each invocation triggers expensive NEAR cross-contract calls costing 10–100 Tgas. An unprivileged EVM user can cheaply spam exit calls, imposing disproportionate NEAR gas costs on relayers and causing legitimate exit transactions to be delayed or blocked.

### Finding Description

Both exit precompiles declare their EVM gas cost as zero via a `TODO` placeholder:

```rust

    fn required_gas(_input: &[u8]) -> Result<EthGas, ExitError> {
        Ok(costs::EXIT_TO_ETHEREUM_GAS)  // always 0
    }
``` [3](#0-2) 

Despite costing 0 EVM gas, each invocation schedules expensive NEAR cross-contract calls. `ExitToNear` attaches `FT_TRANSFER_GAS` (10 Tgas) or `FT_TRANSFER_CALL_GAS` (70 Tgas) plus `EXIT_TO_NEAR_CALLBACK_GAS` (10 Tgas): [4](#0-3) 

`ExitToEthereum` attaches `WITHDRAWAL_GAS` (100 Tgas): [5](#0-4) 

These NEAR gas costs are prepaid by the NEAR account that calls `submit` on the Aurora Engine (the relayer). The EVM gas fees the relayer collects from the EVM sender do not cover the NEAR gas spent on the triggered promises, because the precompile charges 0 EVM gas.

Contrast this with the XCC precompile, which correctly converts NEAR gas costs into EVM gas using `CROSS_CONTRACT_CALL_NEAR_GAS`: [6](#0-5) 

No such conversion exists for the exit precompiles.

### Impact Explanation

**High — Temporary freezing of funds.**

An attacker submits many EVM transactions calling `ExitToNear` or `ExitToEthereum` with dust amounts (1 wei). Each transaction costs only the base 21,000 EVM gas (the precompile itself costs 0). Each transaction forces the relayer to prepay 10–100 Tgas of NEAR gas for the triggered `ft_transfer`, `ft_transfer_call`, or `withdraw` cross-contract calls, receiving zero EVM fee compensation for that portion.

As the relayer's NEAR gas budget is drained without adequate EVM fee recovery, the relayer becomes economically unviable and stops processing exit transactions. Legitimate users who rely on relayers to bridge tokens out of Aurora (to NEAR or Ethereum) find their exit transactions unprocessed. Their bridged funds remain locked inside Aurora until either the relayer resumes or the user finds an alternative submission path — constituting a temporary freeze of in-motion funds.

### Likelihood Explanation

**High.** The attack requires only a funded EVM address on Aurora and the ability to submit EVM transactions. No special privileges, governance access, or leaked keys are needed. The attack is fully automatable: a script can continuously submit dust-value exit calls at near-zero EVM cost. On Aurora, where EVM gas prices can be zero, the attacker's cost approaches zero entirely.

### Recommendation

Assign non-zero EVM gas costs to both exit precompiles that accurately reflect the NEAR gas consumed by the triggered cross-contract calls, following the same methodology used for the XCC precompile (convert NEAR gas to EVM gas via `CROSS_CONTRACT_CALL_NEAR_GAS`). Resolve the open `TODO(#483)`. At minimum:

- `EXIT_TO_NEAR_GAS` should account for `FT_TRANSFER_GAS` (10 Tgas) or `FT_TRANSFER_CALL_GAS` (70 Tgas) plus `EXIT_TO_NEAR_CALLBACK_GAS` (10 Tgas), converted to EVM gas.
- `EXIT_TO_ETHEREUM_GAS` should account for `WITHDRAWAL_GAS` (100 Tgas), converted to EVM gas.

### Proof of Concept

1. Attacker holds 1 wei of ETH (or 1 unit of any bridged ERC-20) on Aurora.
2. Attacker submits N EVM transactions, each calling `ExitToNear` (flag `0x00`) with `apparent_value = 1 wei` and a valid NEAR recipient. EVM gas cost per call: 21,000 (base) + 0 (precompile) = 21,000 gas.
3. Each transaction, when processed by the relayer via `submit`, triggers an `ft_transfer` promise on the ETH connector with `FT_TRANSFER_GAS = 10 Tgas` prepaid from the relayer's NEAR gas budget.
4. The relayer receives 0 EVM gas fee compensation for the 10 Tgas spent per exit call.
5. After N iterations, the relayer's NEAR gas expenditure far exceeds EVM fee income; the relayer halts exit transaction processing.
6. Legitimate users attempting to exit tokens from Aurora find their transactions unprocessed; their funds are temporarily frozen inside Aurora. [7](#0-6) [8](#0-7)
