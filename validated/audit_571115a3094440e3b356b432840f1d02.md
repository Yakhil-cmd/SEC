### Title
SNS `perform_manage_ledger_parameters` Fails to Update `transaction_fee_e8s` on Upgrade Confirmation Timeout, Creating Persistent Fee Discrepancy — (File: `rs/sns/governance/src/governance.rs`)

---

### Summary

When a `ManageLedgerParameters` proposal changes the SNS ledger's `transfer_fee`, the SNS governance canister upgrades the ledger and then polls `canister_info` to confirm the upgrade. The governance's cached `transaction_fee_e8s` is only updated upon successful confirmation. If the polling loop times out (after 5 minutes), the ledger has the new fee but governance permanently retains the old `transaction_fee_e8s`. This creates a persistent discrepancy between individual neuron operation fee calculations (using the stale governance parameter) and the actual ledger fee — directly analogous to the original `setGasCompensation()` bug where changing `USDE_GAS_COMPENSATION` retroactively affected all troves' ICR without compensating the gas pool (TCR).

---

### Finding Description

In `perform_manage_ledger_parameters`, the execution flow is:

1. The ledger is upgraded with the new `transfer_fee` via `upgrade_non_root_canister` [1](#0-0) 

2. A polling loop checks `canister_info` to confirm the upgrade completed [2](#0-1) 

3. **Only on successful confirmation** does it update `nervous_system_parameters.transaction_fee_e8s` [3](#0-2) 

4. If the polling times out after 5 minutes, the function returns `Err` **without** updating `transaction_fee_e8s` [4](#0-3) 

After the timeout, the ledger has the new `transfer_fee` but governance's `transaction_fee_e8s` is permanently stale. All subsequent neuron operations that call `transaction_fee_e8s_or_panic()` will calculate amounts based on the wrong fee. [5](#0-4) 

The stale `transaction_fee_e8s` is used in:

- **`disburse_neuron`**: subtracts `transaction_fee_e8s` from the disburse amount and deducts it from `cached_neuron_stake_e8s` [6](#0-5) 
- **`split_neuron`**: validates `split.amount_e8s < min_stake + transaction_fee_e8s` [7](#0-6) 
- **`disburse_to_neuron`** (NNS): uses `transaction_fee_e8s` for amount validation and child neuron creation [8](#0-7) 

The `ManageLedgerParameters` proposal type is a standard SNS governance action reachable by any SNS neuron holder. [9](#0-8) 

The `NervousSystemParameters.transaction_fee_e8s` field is explicitly documented as the governance-side mirror of the ledger's fee: [10](#0-9) 

---

### Impact Explanation

**If the new ledger fee is higher than the old fee:** Governance operations will attempt ledger transfers with an insufficient fee argument. The ledger will reject these transfers, causing neuron disbursals and splits to fail for all users until the discrepancy is manually resolved via another governance proposal.

**If the new ledger fee is lower than the old fee:** Governance will over-deduct from neurons — charging users the old (higher) fee in `cached_neuron_stake_e8s` accounting while the ledger only charges the new (lower) fee. Users permanently lose the difference on every disbursal or split.

The discrepancy persists indefinitely until another successful `ManageLedgerParameters` execution updates `transaction_fee_e8s`. [11](#0-10) 

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**Medium-low.** The trigger requires:

1. An SNS governance vote adopting a `ManageLedgerParameters` proposal — a normal, permissionless governance operation available to any neuron holder. [12](#0-11) 
2. The ledger upgrade to succeed but the `canister_info` polling to time out within 5 minutes.

The timeout can occur due to subnet congestion, the `canister_info` 20-change window being exceeded by concurrent canister operations on the same subnet, or the ledger upgrade taking longer than expected. These are realistic operational conditions on a loaded subnet.

---

### Recommendation

Update `nervous_system_parameters.transaction_fee_e8s` **immediately after `upgrade_non_root_canister` returns `Ok`**, before entering the polling loop. This ensures the governance parameter is always consistent with the ledger fee regardless of whether the polling confirmation succeeds or times out. The polling loop can remain for proposal status tracking purposes, but the fee update must not be gated on it.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. SNS neuron holder submits a `ManageLedgerParameters` proposal to increase `transfer_fee` from 10,000 to 100,000 e8s. [13](#0-12) 
2. Proposal is adopted by governance vote.
3. `perform_manage_ledger_parameters` executes:
   - `upgrade_non_root_canister` succeeds — ledger now has `transfer_fee = 100,000 e8s`.
   - `canister_info` polling times out after 5 minutes (e.g., subnet congestion).
   - `transaction_fee_e8s` is **not** updated — governance still stores `10,000 e8s`.
4. A neuron holder calls `disburse_neuron`:
   - Governance computes `disburse_amount_e8s -= transaction_fee_e8s` using `10,000 e8s`. [14](#0-13) 
   - Governance calls the ledger with `fee = 10,000 e8s`.
   - The ledger rejects the transfer: expected fee is `100,000 e8s`.
   - The disbursal fails; the neuron is locked.

Alternatively, if the fee was decreased from 100,000 to 10,000 e8s and the timeout occurs:
4. Governance computes `disburse_amount_e8s -= 100,000 e8s` (old fee) and deducts `100,000 e8s` from `cached_neuron_stake_e8s`. [15](#0-14) 
5. The ledger charges only `10,000 e8s`.
6. The neuron holder loses `90,000 e8s` more than expected on every disbursal — a permanent, silent token loss.
