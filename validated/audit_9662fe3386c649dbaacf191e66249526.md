Audit Report

## Title
Excess ICP Permanently Locked in Buyer Subaccount During Open Swap - (File: rs/sns/swap/src/swap.rs)

## Summary
The `refresh_buyer_token_e8s` function in the SNS Swap canister caps accepted ICP at per-participant and global limits but does not return the excess ICP to the buyer. The excess remains locked in the buyer's subaccount of the Swap canister for the entire remaining duration of the open swap — up to 90 days — because the only recovery mechanism, `error_refund_icp`, is gated behind the swap reaching `Aborted` or `Committed` state. Any unprivileged buyer who sends more ICP than either cap will have their excess funds inaccessible for the swap's duration.

## Finding Description
In `rs/sns/swap/src/swap.rs`, `refresh_buyer_token_e8s` reads the buyer's ICP balance from the ledger (`e8s`), then computes a capped `new_balance_e8s` via two sequential `min` operations:

1. `actual_increment_e8s = min(max_increment_e8s, requested_increment_e8s)` — caps at remaining global direct participation capacity.
2. `new_balance_e8s = min(new_balance_e8s, max_participant_icp_e8s)` — caps at the per-participant limit. [1](#0-0) 

After capping, the function records only `new_balance_e8s` in the buyer's state and returns `Ok(RefreshBuyerTokensResponse { icp_accepted_participation_e8s: new_balance_e8s, icp_ledger_account_balance_e8s: e8s })` — the difference `e8s - new_balance_e8s` is never transferred back. [2](#0-1) 

The code itself acknowledges this with an open TODO: [3](#0-2) 

The only recovery path, `error_refund_icp`, explicitly rejects calls while the swap is open: [4](#0-3) 

The externally callable entry point is `refresh_buyer_tokens` in `rs/sns/swap/canister/canister.rs`, callable by any unprivileged principal: [5](#0-4) 

## Impact Explanation
This is a **High** severity finding matching: *"Significant SNS security impact with concrete user or protocol harm."* Any buyer who sends more ICP than `max_participant_icp_e8s` or more than `available_direct_participation_e8s()` has their excess ICP locked in the Swap canister's subaccount for the full remaining duration of the open swap (up to 90 days per SNS parameters). The funds are not permanently lost — they are recoverable via `error_refund_icp` after the swap closes — but they are completely inaccessible during the swap, preventing the buyer from staking, participating in other swaps, or otherwise using those tokens. During popular SNS launches with large participation, this can lock up substantial ICP amounts across many users simultaneously.

## Likelihood Explanation
This is highly likely to be triggered in practice. The conditions are routine: any buyer who sends more than `max_participant_icp_e8s`, or any buyer who participates when `available_direct_participation_e8s()` is less than their transfer amount (i.e., the swap is nearly full), will trigger the lock-up. No special privileges are required — the attack path is a standard ingress call to `refresh_buyer_tokens` by any principal. The SNS swap UI encourages buyers to send their maximum desired participation amount, making over-cap transfers a common occurrence during popular launches.

## Recommendation
Inside `refresh_buyer_token_e8s`, after computing `new_balance_e8s`, calculate the excess (`e8s - new_balance_e8s`) and immediately initiate an ICP ledger transfer back to the buyer's principal account before returning. The existing `error_refund_icp` logic at lines 1971–2004 demonstrates the correct transfer pattern (`transfer_funds` from `principal_to_subaccount(buyer)` back to the buyer's principal account). The open TODO at line 1133 (`TODO(NNS1-1682): attempt to refund ICP that cannot be accepted`) confirms this is the intended fix. [6](#0-5) 

## Proof of Concept
1. Deploy a local SNS swap with `max_participant_icp_e8s = 5 ICP` and configure the swap so `available_direct_participation_e8s() = 3 ICP` (swap nearly full).
2. Buyer transfers 5 ICP to `subaccount(swap_canister_id, buyer_principal)` on the ICP ledger.
3. Buyer calls `refresh_buyer_tokens` (ingress, no privileges required).
4. `refresh_buyer_token_e8s` reads `e8s = 5 ICP`, computes `actual_increment_e8s = min(3, 5) = 3 ICP`, then `new_balance_e8s = min(3, 5) = 3 ICP`. Records 3 ICP for the buyer.
5. Function returns `Ok(RefreshBuyerTokensResponse { icp_accepted_participation_e8s: 3, icp_ledger_account_balance_e8s: 5 })`. The 2 ICP excess is not returned.
6. Buyer immediately calls `error_refund_icp` — receives: `"Error refunds can only be performed when the swap is ABORTED or COMMITTED"`.
7. Buyer's 2 ICP excess remains locked in `subaccount(swap_canister_id, buyer_principal)` until the swap closes. Verify via a PocketIC integration test asserting the subaccount balance equals 2 ICP and `error_refund_icp` returns a precondition error while the swap is in `Open` state.
