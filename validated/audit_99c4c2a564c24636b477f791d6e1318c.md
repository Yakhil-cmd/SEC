Audit Report

## Title
Unprivileged Caller Can Dilute Neuron Age Bonus via Unauthenticated `ClaimOrRefresh { by: NeuronIdOrSubaccount }` - (File: rs/nns/governance/src/governance.rs)

## Summary
Any unprivileged principal can reduce a victim NNS neuron's age bonus — and therefore its voting power — by transferring ICP to the neuron's subaccount and then calling `manage_neuron` with `ClaimOrRefresh { by: NeuronIdOrSubaccount }`. The `refresh_neuron_by_id_or_subaccount` dispatch path performs no caller authorization check. When the ledger balance exceeds the cached stake, `update_stake_adjust_age` is invoked unconditionally, computing a weighted-average age that moves `aging_since_timestamp_seconds` forward in time. The attack is repeatable, permanent without years of waiting, and requires no privileged access.

## Finding Description

**No caller authorization at the entry point.**
`refresh_neuron_by_id_or_subaccount` (L5875–5896) resolves the neuron from either a `NeuronId` or a `Subaccount` and immediately delegates to `refresh_neuron`. Neither this function nor its caller in the `manage_neuron` dispatch chain checks that the caller is the neuron's controller or a hot key.

**`refresh_neuron` unconditionally calls `update_stake_adjust_age` when balance > cached stake.**
At L5950–5951, the `Ordering::Less` branch (balance > cached stake) calls `neuron.update_stake_adjust_age(balance.get_e8s(), now)` with no guard on caller identity.

**`update_stake_adjust_age` dilutes the age via weighted average.**
At L1021–1026 in `rs/nns/governance/src/neuron/types.rs`, `combine_aged_stakes` is called with the added portion carrying `age = 0`. This produces a new `aging_since_timestamp_seconds = now - (old_stake * old_age) / (old_stake + added_stake)`, permanently moving the timestamp forward.

**Only `NotDissolving` neurons are affected — exactly the ones that accumulate age bonus.**
`adjust_age` in `rs/nns/governance/src/neuron/dissolve_state_and_age.rs` (L255–268) is a no-op for `DissolvingOrDissolved` neurons, so the attack targets only neurons actively accumulating the age bonus (up to +25% voting power at ≥4 years).

**Existing guards are insufficient.**
The only guard in `refresh_neuron` is a minimum-balance check (L5924–5934), which the attacker satisfies by design. There is no check that the caller owns or controls the neuron.

## Impact Explanation

This is a **High** severity finding matching: *"Unauthorized access to neurons, governance assets… where exploitation requires meaningful per-target work or other constraints."*

An attacker can selectively reduce the voting power of specific neurons — for example, neurons known to vote against the attacker's preferred governance proposals — without any privileged access. A neuron with 100 ICP staked for 4 years that receives an unsolicited 100 ICP top-up will have its effective age halved to 2 years, losing half its age bonus. The victim cannot undo this: the ICP is locked in their neuron, and the age bonus cannot be recovered without waiting years. The attack is repeatable: continuous small transfers keep the neuron's age near zero indefinitely.

## Likelihood Explanation

The attack requires only: (1) knowledge of the victim's neuron ID (public via `list_neurons`), (2) ability to compute the neuron's subaccount (deterministic from neuron ID), (3) a small ICP balance to transfer, and (4) a single unauthenticated `manage_neuron` ingress call. The ICP is not lost to the attacker — it is locked in the victim's neuron — making the cost low relative to the governance impact. No privileged role, leaked key, or social engineering is required.

## Recommendation

1. **Require caller authorization for the `NeuronIdOrSubaccount` path**: Before calling `refresh_neuron`, verify that the caller is the neuron's controller or a registered hot key, consistent with how other neuron-mutating commands are gated.
2. **Separate stake update from age update**: When a refresh is triggered by a non-owner (e.g., a third party who sent ICP), update `cached_neuron_stake_e8s` to reflect the true balance but do not adjust `aging_since_timestamp_seconds`. Age should only be recalculated when the owner explicitly acknowledges the new stake.
3. **Minimum added-stake threshold**: Require the balance increase to exceed `neuron_minimum_stake_e8s` before triggering an age adjustment, to deter dust-based repeated attacks.

## Proof of Concept

```rust
// Attacker knows victim neuron_id N, subaccount S, stake 100 ICP, age 4 years.

// Step 1: Transfer ICP to victim's neuron subaccount.
ledger.transfer({
    to: AccountIdentifier::new(GOVERNANCE_CANISTER_ID, Some(S)),
    amount: 100_ICP,
    fee: DEFAULT_FEE,
    memo: 0,
    from_subaccount: None,
});

// Step 2: Call manage_neuron as any principal — no auth check.
governance.manage_neuron(attacker_principal, ManageNeuron {
    neuron_id_or_subaccount: Some(NeuronIdOrSubaccount::NeuronId(N)),
    id: None,
    command: Some(Command::ClaimOrRefresh(ClaimOrRefresh {
        by: Some(By::NeuronIdOrSubaccount(Empty {})),
    })),
});

// Result: balance (200 ICP) > cached_stake (100 ICP)
// => update_stake_adjust_age(200 ICP, now) called
// => new_age = (100 ICP * 4 years) / 200 ICP = 2 years
// => aging_since_timestamp_seconds reset to now - 2_years
// Victim's age bonus halved without consent.
```

A unit test using `PocketIC` or the existing governance test harness can reproduce this by: (a) creating a neuron with a known stake and age, (b) directly crediting the neuron's subaccount on the mock ledger, (c) calling `manage_neuron` from an unrelated principal with `ClaimOrRefresh { by: NeuronIdOrSubaccount }`, and (d) asserting that `aging_since_timestamp_seconds` has advanced.
