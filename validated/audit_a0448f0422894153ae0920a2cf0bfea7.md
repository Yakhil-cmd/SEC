### Title
Low `neuron_management_fee_per_proposal_e8s` Enables Global ManageNeuron Proposal Queue Exhaustion DOS - (File: rs/nns/governance/src/governance.rs)

---

### Summary

The NNS governance canister charges a small, fixed, non-refundable fee (`neuron_management_fee_per_proposal_e8s`, 0.01 ICP) for every `ManageNeuron` proposal regardless of outcome. A global cap on open `ManageNeuron` proposals is enforced with a subnet-wide counter and no per-neuron sub-limit. An unprivileged neuron holder can repeatedly submit `ManageNeuron` proposals targeting their own neuron at minimal cost, exhausting the global queue and blocking all legitimate `ManageNeuron` proposals across the NNS.

---

### Finding Description

In `rs/nns/governance/src/governance.rs`, `make_proposal` dispatches to `proposal_submission_fee` to determine the upfront charge: [1](#0-0) 

For `ManageNeuron` actions this returns `neuron_management_fee_per_proposal_e8s` (0.01 ICP). The companion `reject_cost_e8s` function explicitly sets the refund to **zero** for `ManageNeuron` proposals: [2](#0-1) 

The fee is then deducted from the proposer's neuron stake unconditionally: [3](#0-2) 

The only guard against queue exhaustion is a **global** (not per-neuron) count of open `ManageNeuron` proposals: [4](#0-3) 

There is no per-neuron sub-limit. An attacker who controls a neuron (or several neurons) can submit `ManageNeuron` proposals targeting their own neuron (e.g., repeatedly changing followees on `Topic::NeuronManagement`) without voting on them, keeping them in `Open` status until they expire. Each proposal costs only 0.01 ICP. Once `MAX_NUMBER_OF_OPEN_MANAGE_NEURON_PROPOSALS` is reached, every subsequent `ManageNeuron` proposal from any user is rejected with `ResourceExhausted`.

The `neuron_management_fee_per_proposal_e8s` field is part of `NetworkEconomics`: [5](#0-4) 

The attack mirrors the external report exactly: a small, always-consumed fee (analogous to `govFee`) is charged per attempt; the shared resource (the global `ManageNeuron` proposal queue, analogous to oracle node capacity) is exhausted; and the attack continues until the attacker's stake is depleted, at which point the attacker simply tops up and resumes.

---

### Impact Explanation

**Medium.** When `MAX_NUMBER_OF_OPEN_MANAGE_NEURON_PROPOSALS` is exhausted, no NNS participant can submit a `ManageNeuron` proposal. This blocks all neuron-management operations (changing followees, adding/removing hot keys, merging neurons, etc.) for the entire duration of the attack. Legitimate governance participants are denied service while the attacker's proposals remain open.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**High.** Any principal who holds a neuron with stake ≥ `MAX_NUMBER_OF_OPEN_MANAGE_NEURON_PROPOSALS × 0.01 ICP` can execute this attack. The attack is cheap, requires no privileged access, and can be sustained by periodically topping up the neuron. The condition that triggers it (submitting proposals and not voting) is trivially reproducible at any time.

---

### Recommendation

1. **Introduce a per-neuron open-proposal sub-limit** for `ManageNeuron` proposals (e.g., cap each neuron at N open proposals) so a single actor cannot exhaust the global quota.
2. **Increase `neuron_management_fee_per_proposal_e8s`** to a level that makes filling the global queue economically prohibitive.
3. **Implement a cooldown** between consecutive `ManageNeuron` proposals from the same neuron, analogous to the recommendation in the external report to restrict traders from requesting `closeTradeMarket` more than once during market closures.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Obtain a neuron with stake ≥ `MAX_NUMBER_OF_OPEN_MANAGE_NEURON_PROPOSALS × 0.01 ICP`.
2. Repeatedly call `make_proposal` with `Action::ManageNeuron` targeting the attacker's own neuron (e.g., `Command::Follow` on `Topic::NeuronManagement`), without casting a vote on any submitted proposal.
3. Each proposal is accepted, deducts 0.01 ICP from `neuron_fees_e8s`, and increments the global open-`ManageNeuron`-proposal counter.
4. Once the counter reaches `MAX_NUMBER_OF_OPEN_MANAGE_NEURON_PROPOSALS`, every subsequent `make_proposal` call from any principal with a `ManageNeuron` action returns `ResourceExhausted`.
5. The attacker's proposals remain open until their voting period expires; the attacker can immediately re-fill the queue after expiry, sustaining the DOS indefinitely at a cost of 0.01 ICP per slot per voting period.
