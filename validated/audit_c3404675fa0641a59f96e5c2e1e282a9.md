### Title
SNS `split_neuron` Does Not Transfer `staked_maturity_e8s_equivalent` to Child Neuron, Inflating Parent Voting Power - (File: `rs/sns/governance/src/governance.rs`)

### Summary

The SNS governance `split_neuron` function updates `cached_neuron_stake_e8s` for both parent and child neurons after a split, but never reduces the parent's `staked_maturity_e8s_equivalent` nor assigns any proportional share to the child. This leaves the parent with 100% of its staked maturity after giving away a portion of its stake, inflating the parent's voting power and zeroing the child's voting power contribution from staked maturity.

### Finding Description

In `rs/sns/governance/src/governance.rs`, the `split_neuron` function creates the child neuron with `staked_maturity_e8s_equivalent: None` hardcoded: [1](#0-0) 

After the ledger transfer succeeds, the post-transfer state update only adjusts `cached_neuron_stake_e8s` for both neurons: [2](#0-1) 

The parent's `staked_maturity_e8s_equivalent` is never reduced, and the child's is never set. This is in direct contrast to the NNS governance `split_neuron`, which explicitly reads the parent's staked maturity after the ledger call, calculates a proportional transfer via `calculate_split_neuron_effect`, decreases the parent's `staked_maturity_e8s_equivalent`, and increases the child's: [3](#0-2) [4](#0-3) 

The SNS neuron's `voting_power_stake_e8s()` explicitly includes `staked_maturity_e8s_equivalent` in the voting power calculation: [5](#0-4) 

So after a split, the parent retains 100% of its staked maturity despite having transferred a portion of its stake, and the child receives 0 staked maturity despite inheriting a proportional share of the economic value.

### Impact Explanation

**Governance accounting bug / voting power inflation.** Any SNS neuron holder with `staked_maturity_e8s_equivalent > 0` who calls `split_neuron` causes:

1. **Parent voting power inflation**: The parent retains all staked maturity even though its stake decreased. Its `voting_power_stake_e8s` = `(cached_stake - fees) + full_staked_maturity`, which is disproportionately high relative to its actual economic stake.
2. **Child voting power deflation**: The child gets `staked_maturity_e8s_equivalent = None`, so its voting power is computed only from its `cached_neuron_stake_e8s`, underrepresenting its true economic weight.
3. **Total SNS voting power inflation**: The sum of parent + child voting power exceeds what it would be if staked maturity were proportionally distributed, because the parent's staked maturity is double-counted relative to the split stake.

This directly corrupts SNS governance vote tallying for any SNS that has neurons with staked maturity that have been split.

### Likelihood Explanation

**Medium.** The `split_neuron` operation is a standard, permissionless user action callable by any principal holding the `NeuronPermissionType::Split` permission on their own neuron. Any SNS user who has accumulated `staked_maturity_e8s_equivalent` (via voting rewards with `auto_stake_maturity = true`) and then splits their neuron triggers this bug. The SNS `auto_stake_maturity` feature is documented and actively used, making this a realistic scenario. [6](#0-5) 

### Recommendation

After a successful ledger transfer in `split_neuron`, proportionally transfer `staked_maturity_e8s_equivalent` from parent to child, mirroring the NNS governance implementation. Specifically:

1. Read the parent's current `staked_maturity_e8s_equivalent` after the ledger call.
2. Compute `transfer_staked_maturity = parent_staked_maturity * split_amount_e8s / parent_stake_e8s`.
3. Subtract `transfer_staked_maturity` from the parent's `staked_maturity_e8s_equivalent`.
4. Set the child's `staked_maturity_e8s_equivalent` to `transfer_staked_maturity` (if > 0).

### Proof of Concept

1. An SNS user enables `auto_stake_maturity` on their neuron and accumulates voting rewards, resulting in `staked_maturity_e8s_equivalent = X`.
2. The user calls `manage_neuron` with `Command::Split { amount_e8s: half_stake, memo: 0 }`.
3. After the split:
   - Parent neuron: `cached_neuron_stake_e8s = half_stake`, `staked_maturity_e8s_equivalent = X` (unchanged — should be `X/2`).
   - Child neuron: `cached_neuron_stake_e8s = half_stake - fee`, `staked_maturity_e8s_equivalent = None` (should be `X/2`).
4. Parent's `voting_power_stake_e8s()` = `half_stake + X` instead of the correct `half_stake + X/2`.
5. Child's `voting_power_stake_e8s()` = `half_stake - fee` instead of the correct `half_stake - fee + X/2`.
6. Total voting power across parent + child is inflated by `X/2` compared to the pre-split state. [7](#0-6) [8](#0-7)
