Audit Report

## Title
Stale ICP/XDR Exchange Rate Used in Cycles Minting Without Age Validation - (File: `rs/nns/cmc/src/main.rs`)

## Summary
The Cycles Minting Canister stores an ICP/XDR conversion rate that is never validated for freshness at the point of use. `tokens_to_cycles()` only guards against a `None` rate, not a stale one. When the Exchange Rate Canister (XRC) is unavailable, the CMC retries every minute but never invalidates the stored rate, allowing any unprivileged caller to mint cycles at an arbitrarily outdated rate.

## Finding Description
`tokens_to_cycles()` at `rs/nns/cmc/src/main.rs:1900–1923` reads `state.icp_xdr_conversion_rate` and extracts only `xdr_permyriad_per_icp`, with no check on `rate.timestamp_seconds` relative to the current canister time. The sole guard is a `None` match.

`do_set_icp_xdr_conversion_rate()` at `rs/nns/cmc/src/main.rs:1022–1030` enforces only monotonicity: the new rate's timestamp must exceed the stored one. There is no check that the stored rate is within a tolerable window of wall-clock time.

`validate_exchange_rate()` in `rs/nervous_system/clients/src/exchange_rate_canister_client.rs:111–129` checks only `base_asset_num_received_rates` and `quote_asset_num_received_rates`. No timestamp freshness check is performed before accepting a rate into state.

When the XRC call fails, `UpdateExchangeRateGuard::schedule_next_attempt()` at `rs/nns/cmc/src/exchange_rate_canister.rs:133–165` schedules a retry at the next minute interval but does not clear or invalidate the stored rate. The stored rate silently ages with no upper bound.

All three public minting entry points — `notify_top_up` (`#[update]`, L1139), `notify_create_canister`, and `notify_mint_cycles` — call `tokens_to_cycles()` and are reachable by any unprivileged ingress sender.

## Impact Explanation
This enables illegal minting of cycles. If the XRC is unavailable while the ICP market price falls, the stored rate remains elevated. Any caller can invoke `notify_top_up` with ICP and receive cycles computed at the stale (higher) rate, extracting more cycles per ICP than the current market rate justifies. This constitutes unauthorized minting of cycles from the IC network, mapping to the Critical/High allowed impact: "Theft, permanent loss, illegal minting, or protocol insolvency involving exorbitant ICP/Cycles." The magnitude scales with the duration of XRC unavailability and the degree of ICP price movement during that window.

## Likelihood Explanation
XRC unavailability is a realistic and explicitly anticipated operational scenario — the CMC's own retry logic and integration tests (`rs/nns/integration_tests/src/cycles_minting_canister_with_exchange_rate_canister.rs:162–185`) exercise the case where the XRC returns errors across multiple consecutive 5-minute windows. No special privileges are required: `notify_top_up` is a public `#[update]` method. An attacker who observes that the XRC is down and that the stored rate is favorable (ICP price has since dropped) can immediately and repeatedly call `notify_top_up` until the XRC recovers and the rate is updated. The attack requires no victim interaction and is repeatable for the full duration of the outage.

## Recommendation
1. **Add a staleness check in `tokens_to_cycles()`**: Compare `rate.timestamp_seconds` against `env.now_timestamp_seconds()`. If the age exceeds a configurable maximum (e.g., 30–60 minutes), return `NotifyError::Other` rather than minting at a stale rate.
2. **Add a timestamp freshness check in `validate_exchange_rate()`**: After receiving a rate from the XRC, verify that `exchange_rate.timestamp` is within a reasonable window of the current canister time before accepting it into state.
3. **Consider invalidating the stored rate** after a configurable maximum staleness period in the heartbeat/retry path, so that minting is suspended rather than proceeding at an unboundedly old rate.

## Proof of Concept
1. Deploy CMC and mock XRC locally (as done in `rs/nns/integration_tests/src/cycles_minting_canister_with_exchange_rate_canister.rs`).
2. Allow the CMC to store a rate at time T (e.g., `xdr_permyriad_per_icp = 100_000`, i.e., 10 XDR/ICP).
3. Configure the mock XRC to return `ExchangeRateError::StablecoinRateTooFewRates` for all subsequent calls.
4. Advance simulated time by several hours. Confirm via `get_icp_xdr_conversion_rate` that the stored rate is unchanged and its `timestamp_seconds` is now hours in the past.
5. Call `notify_top_up` with 1 ICP. Observe that `tokens_to_cycles()` returns cycles computed at the stale `100_000` permyriad rate with no error.
6. Assert that the returned cycles exceed what the current (lower) market rate would justify — demonstrating overminting with no age guard at the call site.
