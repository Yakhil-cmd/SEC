### Title
Relayer Reward Theft via Front-Running of `submit()` / `submit_with_args()` — (File: `engine/src/contract_methods/evm_transactions.rs`)

---

### Summary

The Aurora Engine awards the EVM priority-fee tip (relayer reward) to whichever NEAR account calls `submit()` or `submit_with_args()`. Because the signed Ethereum transaction bytes carry no cryptographic binding to the NEAR-level submitter, any attacker who observes a pending `submit()` call in the NEAR mempool can re-submit the identical EVM transaction bytes from their own NEAR account with a higher NEAR gas price, arriving first and stealing the relayer reward.

---

### Finding Description

**Reward derivation — no binding to the original submitter**

In both `submit()` and `submit_with_args()`, the relayer address that will receive the EVM tip is computed exclusively from the NEAR `predecessor_account_id`:

```rust
// engine/src/contract_methods/evm_transactions.rs  line 84 / 116
let relayer_address = predecessor_address(&env.predecessor_account_id());
```

`predecessor_address` is a deterministic hash of the NEAR account name:

```rust
// engine/src/contract_methods/mod.rs  line 113-115
fn predecessor_address(predecessor_account_id: &AccountId) -> Address {
    aurora_engine_sdk::types::near_account_to_evm_address(predecessor_account_id.as_bytes())
}
```

After EVM execution, `refund_unused_gas` credits the tip to that address:

```rust
// engine/src/engine.rs  line 1284, 1298-1300
let reward_amount = gas_to_wei(gas_result.priority_fee_per_gas)?;
...
if !relayer_reward.is_zero() {
    add_balance(io, relayer, relayer_reward)?;
}
```

The reward is `priority_fee_per_gas × gas_used` — proportional to the gas consumed by the EVM transaction, which in turn scales with the complexity/size of the call. High-value EVM transactions (large token transfers, complex DeFi interactions) therefore carry large tips and are the most attractive targets.

**The signed EVM transaction bytes are freely replayable at the NEAR level**

The Ethereum transaction is ECDSA-signed by the EVM sender and contains a fixed nonce, gas price, and payload. The Aurora Engine validates the EVM signature and nonce, but it places **no restriction on which NEAR account may submit those bytes**. Any NEAR account can call `submit(same_tx_bytes)` and become the relayer.

---

### Impact Explanation

An attacker who front-runs a `submit()` call receives the full EVM priority-fee tip (`priority_fee_per_gas × gas_used`) that was intended for the legitimate relayer. The legitimate relayer's NEAR gas cost is wasted and their expected reward is stolen. This constitutes **theft of unclaimed yield** (the relayer fee) from the relayer.

Because the reward scales with `gas_used` (and thus with the complexity and value of the underlying EVM transaction), high-value transactions are disproportionately targeted, making the attack economically rational for any transaction whose tip exceeds the cost of the NEAR front-run transaction.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

NEAR Protocol orders transactions within a chunk by gas price. An attacker who monitors the NEAR RPC mempool, extracts the raw EVM bytes from a pending `submit()` call, and resubmits them with a marginally higher NEAR gas price will reliably be included before the victim. No special privilege, key, or admin access is required — only a standard NEAR account and mempool visibility. The attack is fully permissionless and repeatable.

---

### Recommendation

Bind the relayer reward to a NEAR account that is either:

1. **Embedded in the EVM transaction itself** — e.g., allow the EVM sender to include a `relayer` field (analogous to `SubmitArgs.max_gas_price`) that is signed over, so only the designated NEAR account can claim the tip; or
2. **Verified via a relayer registry** — require that the submitting NEAR account is a registered relayer key (the `add_relayer_key` / `store_relayer_key_callback` infrastructure already exists) and reject `submit()` calls from unregistered accounts when a non-zero tip is present; or
3. **Reduce tip to cover only NEAR gas cost** — cap `priority_fee_per_gas` rewards so that front-running yields no profit above the cost of the NEAR transaction itself.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Alice (EVM address `0xAlice`) signs an EVM transaction with `max_priority_fee_per_gas = 100 gwei` and `gas_limit = 200_000`. She gives the RLP-encoded bytes to a legitimate relayer (`relay.near`).
2. `relay.near` broadcasts `submit(rlp_bytes)` to the NEAR network.
3. Attacker (`attacker.near`) observes the pending receipt, extracts `rlp_bytes`, and broadcasts `submit(rlp_bytes)` from `attacker.near` with a higher NEAR gas price.
4. `attacker.near`'s receipt is included first. The engine computes:
   - `relayer_address = near_account_to_evm_address(b"attacker.near")`
   - `relayer_reward = 100 gwei × gas_used`
   - `add_balance(io, relayer_address, relayer_reward)` — attacker's EVM address is credited.
5. When `relay.near`'s receipt arrives, it fails with `ERR_INCORRECT_NONCE` (Alice's nonce was already consumed), so `relay.near` receives nothing and wasted its NEAR gas.
